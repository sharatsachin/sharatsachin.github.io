## Machine Learning

- Grew out of work in AI
- New capability for computers, science of getting computers to act without being explicitly programmed.
- Computers make data driven predictions and decisions
- Build a model from sample inputs

### Examples of real life uses:

- Database mining - Large datasets from growth of automation/web.
E.g., Web click data, medical records, biology, engineering
- Applications can’t program by hand.
E.g., Autonomous helicopter, handwriting recognition, most of Natural Language Processing (NLP), Computer Vision.
- Self-customizing programs
E.g., Amazon, Netflix product recommendations
- Understanding human learning (brain, real AI).

### Definitions
* By Arthur Samuel (1959). 
Field of study that gives computers the ability to learn without being explicitly programmed.
* By Tom Mitchell (1998) 
Well-posed learning problem: A computer program is said to learn from experience E with respect to some task T and some performance measure P, if its performance on T, as measured by P, improves with experience E.

## Types 
The major types of ML algorithms are:
### 1. Supervised learning
The computer is presented with example inputs and their desired outputs, given by a "teacher", and the goal is to learn a general rule that maps inputs to outputs.
Examples:
* housing price prediction 
    ![](https://i.imgur.com/2t85kp4.png)
    * example of regression problem
        * about predicting a continuous quantity
    * should we -
        * straight line through data?
        * second order polynomial?
* deciding wether a tumor is malignant or benign
    ![](https://i.imgur.com/ohdSa8l.png)
    * example of classification problem
        * about predicting a discrete label
    * based on -
        * size (1 feature)
        * age, size, clump thickness, uniformity of cell size, uniformity of cell shape. (5 features)

### 2. Unsupervised learning
No labels are given to the learning algorithm, leaving it on its own to find structure in its input.
Examples:
* clustering algorithms
    * used in :
        * genomics
        ![](https://i.imgur.com/lY1LflB.png)
            * algorithm to cluster individuals into types of people 
            * by measuring expression of genes
        * organizing computing clusters
        * social network analysis
        * market segmentation
        * astronomical data analysis
    * example is : 
        * Google news
            * clusters together news stories based on same event
* cocktail party algorithm
    * Lots of overlapping voices - hard to hear what everyone is saying
    ![](https://i.imgur.com/v7Stvix.png)
        * Two people talking
        * Microphones at different distances from speakers
    * Record sightly different versions of the conversation depending on where your microphone is
    * Algorithm processes audio recordings
        * Determines there are two audio sources
        * Separates out the two sources

Other types of ML algorithms include reinforcement learning and recommender systems.
